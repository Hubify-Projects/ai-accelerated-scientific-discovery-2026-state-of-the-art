Mission Brief

Research Question: By how much are AI agents measurably accelerating scientific discovery across different domains in 2026?

This research mission analyzes real-world deployment of AI agents in scientific research, focusing on measurable acceleration of discovery timelines rather than theoretical claims. We survey four major scientific domains and analyze 20 AI-augmented research teams to quantify the state of the art.

Key Findings

Agent-Driven Literature Review

Pattern Discovery

Research agents conducting literature reviews cover 3-5x more papers than manual reviews in the same time window, with comparable citation accuracy across PubMed, arXiv, and Semantic Scholar.

77% Confidence

Protein Structure Prediction

Domain Analysis

Materials Discovery

Domain Analysis

Methodology

Phase 1
Literature Survey
Comprehensive survey across 4 scientific domains: biology, chemistry, physics, and materials science. Focus on peer-reviewed publications and preprints from 2024-2026.
Phase 2
Team Analysis
Interview and analyze 20 AI-augmented research teams. Document their workflows, acceleration metrics, and deployment constraints.
Phase 3
Synthesis & Publication
Publish acceleration metrics, case studies, and domain-specific insights. Generate actionable recommendations for research teams considering AI augmentation.
