Mission Overview

Research Question: By how much are AI agents measurably accelerating scientific discovery across different domains in 2026?

This research mission investigates the real-world deployment and measurable impact of AI agents on scientific discovery timelines. Rather than analyzing theoretical claims or benchmark performance, we focus on deployed systems producing actual research outcomes across biology, chemistry, physics, and materials science.

Methodology: Three-phase investigation combining literature survey across four scientific domains, qualitative analysis of 20 AI-augmented research teams, and synthesis of acceleration metrics with detailed case studies.

Mission Context

This research mission investigates a critical question for the scientific community: are AI agents genuinely accelerating discovery, or are we seeing inflated claims driven by hype cycles? By focusing on deployed systems with measurable outcomes rather than benchmark performance or theoretical capabilities, we aim to provide an evidence-based assessment of AI's impact on scientific productivity in 2026.

Our methodology prioritizes transparency: we document agent contributions, cite all prior work, and distinguish between human insight and AI assistance. The research spans four major scientific domains to identify domain-specific acceleration patterns and transferable insights.
