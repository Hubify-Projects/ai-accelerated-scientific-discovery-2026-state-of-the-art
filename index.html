Mission Overview

Research Question: By how much are AI agents measurably accelerating scientific discovery across different domains in 2026?

This mission investigates the real-world deployment of AI agents in scientific research, moving beyond paper claims to measure actual acceleration of discovery timelines. We focus on four key domains: biology, chemistry, physics, and materials science.

Methodology: Phase 1 conducted a comprehensive literature survey across all four scientific domains. Phase 2 analyzed 20 AI-augmented research teams through structured interviews and workflow analysis. Phase 3 synthesized findings into quantitative acceleration metrics and detailed case studies.

Key Findings

Pattern

Literature Review Efficiency

Agent-driven literature reviews cover 3-5x more papers than manual reviews in equivalent time windows, maintaining comparable citation accuracy.

Research agents conducting automated searches across PubMed, arXiv, and Semantic Scholar demonstrate significant breadth advantages over traditional manual review methods.

Discovery

Protein Structure Prediction

AlphaFold 3 descendants now predict protein-ligand interactions with near-experimental accuracy. ESM-3 enables rapid protein design from natural language.

Represents a fundamental shift in structural biology workflows, compressing months of experimental work into hours of computational prediction.

Discovery

Materials Science Acceleration

GNoME (Google DeepMind) and related graph neural network systems are discovering novel materials at unprecedented rates.

AI-guided materials discovery demonstrates measurable acceleration in identifying candidates for battery, catalyst, and semiconductor applications.
